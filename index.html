ORIGIN AND EVOLUTION OF BIRDS

Birds have long been known to have originated from dinosaurs, which are actually a type of reptile. Abundant finds of feathered dinosaur fossils in China and elsewhere have documented in detail the spectacular transformation of terrestrial giants into graceful flying birds.

New analytical techniques in paleontology have made it possible to reconstruct the evolution of the unique anatomical structures of birds. The results suggest that birds emerged gradually over tens of millions of years for very different purposes than they do today.

When did the first birds appear?

The fossil record shows that birds originated in the Jurassic period, which corresponds to the Mesozoic II period, about 150 million years ago. At the boundary between the Cretaceous and the Paleogene a mass extinction event occurred that affected most of the dinosaurs and feathered animals. However, a group of these survived the event, allowing the evolution of birds and the appearance of today's mammals.

Exotic birds in the American continent

Among the countries that offer the most exotic species in the world, those with tropical forests are in first place, since they are the origin of a large number of these birds. For example, the turpial is the national bird of Venezuela, where the beautiful cardinal is also found.

The quetzal is found in the forests of Guatemala, Mexico and Costa Rica, in the latter, we can also find more than forty species of hummingbirds and several species of toucans.

When we talk about tropical rainforests, we cannot fail to mention Brazil, which has the third largest number of specimens in South America. There we find the Amazon rainforest, where, for example, the pendulum bird is found, a species that also inhabits other parts of Central America and the Caribbean islands.

The hyacinth macaw has fewer and fewer trees to nest in, due to deforestation caused by cattle ranching, fires and the country’s hydrographic plans. Colombia has become an obligatory route for migratory birds in the Americas and with 20% of the world’s bird population, it is a paradise for birdwatchers.

Other parts of the world can also be included, as cockatoos, like parakeets, are native to Australia. The list also includes small birds, such as pigeons, waders, ducks and some gallinaceous birds, whose habitats are spread all over the world.

The different species of tropical birds we find are:

1) Psittaciformes: parakeets, parrots, macaws, cockatoos and lories.
2) Passeriformes: colorful songbirds (goldfinch, finch, canary, oriole).
3) Columbiformes: pigeons, domestic and wild
4) Galliformes: chickens, quails, turkeys or pheasants. 

WHEN DECIDING TO HAVE A BIRD AS A PET

If you wish to own one of these birds as a pet, whether it is a small bird or a large, slow and long-lived one, you must first find out if it is legal to own one and what paperwork is required to make sure that everything is in order and that you are not contributing to the trafficking of exotic animals, some of which are endangered. That is why it is advisable to go to a store, as the sale between individuals could be illegal depending on the laws of your country.

Then you have to learn how to take care of these birds and choose where to keep them at home, the temperature of the room, the cage to use, their diet and how to take care of them so they don’t get sick.

Having a parrot as a pet, you need to have a gentle treatment, because it is a bird that you have to treat with delicacy, really love it, know what species to choose and feed. It is also very sad to see birds abandoned by their owners in urban parks, so be aware of the disadvantages before buying one so as not to regret it later.

You can also keep a breeding pair and it is important to know how to act when they have chicks.

All this information can be found at allaboutbyrds.com, but when you go to the stores, you should inform yourself well to make sure that your birds are happy and live with you for many years as they are long-lived birds and in some cases live as long or longer than humans, such as macaws.

HOW TO BREED BIRDS?

If you are a breeder, but you have more than one pair, you should find out about the conditions of the place where the birds will be, i.e., how much space they need, the temperature, what kind of cage they will use, how much light they will receive, their feeding and watering, if they have a nest and the season in which each species breeds.

Hybridization (crossing of individuals of different species) is more complex than breeding and more difficult to achieve successfully, for example, because the females do not incubate the eggs, they reject the males or the cages, and the chicks may not hatch.

LEGISLATION AND CONSERVATION

Because trade in species, both animals and plants, is a global business, CITES was created in 1973 to ensure that trade is sustainable and does not threaten the survival of species.

Today, almost every country in the world is a signatory to CITES.

Thanks to the organizations that work for the protection of animals and plants, the habitats where these birds live are protected and protected areas are created so that they can live in peace.

THREATS

Other threats are global warming, loss of natural habitats due to indiscriminate logging, forest fires, illegal hunting for trafficking and reproduction. Also, the introduction of predatory species.

On the other hand, females born as a result of hybridization are infertile and do not sing, so they are often unwanted by humans.

The introduction of non-native species is often another threat. This is because species that settle in a new place alter the ecological balance displacing native species.
